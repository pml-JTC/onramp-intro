My Life So Far

A beach bum lost in Manhattan turning darkness into light

A Navy Brat raised on the East Coast

The first things I can remember are crystal blue waters and coral reefs

  • Born in Portsmouth, VA at the Naval Hospital that's been functioning since 1830,
    my father was stationed in Key West when my brain started retaining memories
  • I learned to swim at 2, holding my mother's neck as she swam to the bottom of the deep end
  • At Montessori school I pressed shells into plaster of paris, and grew an avocado
  • We moved back to Virginia--Virginia Beach to be exact--the year I started kindergarten

Mary was my guiding light, and when she died I was lost

As strong as I know my Mother was, her life was too much for her to bear

  • She was a teacher and a natural leader, everyone could depend on her
  • But she really had no one to depend on except her social worker and psychiatrist
    and became overwhelmed and overmedicated--after ECT she was no longer herself
  • When she died, my family was already broken, but now my North Star had burned out

Learn more about suicide prevention

Dreams of being a doctor dashed on the ground

Pride always come before a fall

  • The chaos of my adolescence--losing my Mother, moving from school to school--made it hard to get into my top choice schools
  • At Syracuse University I got off to a very strong start, so strong that I decided to transfer to NYU for stronger academics
  • During the last month of my Freshman year I had a skateboarding accident that resulted in a long-term cognitive difficulty
  • At NYU I felt unable to perform, and didn't want to face that I might struggle with focusing forever
    so I daydreamed as my pre-med dreams faded away

Writing copy for the companies that killed her?

I got my start in medical communications interning as a Copy Department Assistant at a boutique Medical Education firm

  • A girlfriend who was also on all loans at NYU was working as an Editorial Assistant and got me a job as a Copy Assistant
  • Working on staff for a year there after graduating college I was approached by a project manager who told me about Pharma Advertising
  • While a lucrative and intellectually stimulating career, working as a pharmaceutical copywriter felt like I was working for the same industry that filled my mother's hand with pills,
    an industry driven by profit so not incentivized to determine root causes of mental illness
  • Just 24 and already making a good living, but not the one I wanted, I sabotaged my success with heavy drinking and drugs

Self-medication ends in self-destruction

What goes up, must come down

  • My cognitive difficulty seemed to "improve" when I used stimulants, so I began using them all the time
  • I lived a rock star lifestyle and began a cycle of getting hired and fired almost yearly
  • The idea was to work in advertising for a while and go back school, but I ended up homeless
  • On the street I turned to petty crime to stay high, and crime led to prison

Answering the call

Changing perspectives and drawing strength from "weakness"

  • I read a great book in prison that helped me realize that I didn't just have focusing issues, I was severely traumatized
  • Now I am working through my traumas, avoiding drugs and alcohol, and finding my way back to academia
  • In the years since I graduated with a BS in Neural Science, Computational Psychiatry
    has emerged as a truly rigorous approach to deliniating the underlying functional causes of severe mental illnesses
  • The value of lived experience has also become central to efforts to fight stigma and develop effective treatments,
    including non-pharmacological approaches like Brain-Computer Interface and Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation

My hope is to share my lived experience and contribute to the reduction of stigma by applying the coding skills I learn to fields improving mental healthcare
